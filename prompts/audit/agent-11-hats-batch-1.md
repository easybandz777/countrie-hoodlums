**Detailed notes:**
- Material rendering is excellent (same standard as the mascot hat) — leather grain, rivets, burn-tone, hat body all check out.
- Compositional defect: the two crossed implements look like a matched pair of shotguns (similar barrel length, similar muzzle treatment). The brief calls for one shotgun and one fishing rod — the fishing rod should have a visibly different silhouette (reel, line guides, slimmer taper). At thumbnail size and even at full size, no fishing rod is identifiable.
- Cowboy hat at the intersection is present and centered — that part of the brief works.
- "EST. 2024" baseline is present and legible.
- This is a content/brief-mismatch defect, not a rendering defect. The mockup itself is technically high-quality, but it sells the wrong product. Re-render with a clearly differentiated fishing rod (visible reel and line-guide silhouette) crossing a shotgun.

## Summary

| # | File | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `public/images/products/hat-leather-mascot-skull.webp` | **ship** |
| 2 | `public/images/products/hat-leather-crossed-arms.webp` | **re-render** (missing fishing rod) |
| 3 | `public/images/products/hat-leather-sunset-highway.webp` | **re-render** (truck not lifted, no dust plume) |
| 4 | `public/images/products/hat-leather-tree-of-hoodlums.webp` | **ship** |

**Headline findings:**
1. **Rendering quality is consistently high across all four** — leather material, laser-burn tone, hat body color, rivet placement, and white-rectangle avoidance are all clean. No `whiteRectangleDefect` issues in this batch. None of the four had pure-white violations.
2. **Two ships, two re-renders.** Both re-renders are *content/brief-mismatch* defects, not rendering defects — the mockups look like real hats, they just depict the wrong design.
   - Hat #2 (Crew Crest): the fishing rod is missing; both crossed implements read as shotguns.
   - Hat #3 (Sunset Highway): the truck is not lifted and is not kicking dust — neither of the brief's two truck-personality cues is rendered.
3. **Line consistency is good** — patch tone, hat color, mounting style (corner rivets), and patch aspect ratio match across all four. Hat #4's patch tone runs slightly lighter; track at production for stock variance.
4. **Critical risk for hat #2 and #3:** since buyers cannot sample these before purchase, the mockup *is* the promise. Shipping the Crew Crest hat with two shotguns instead of shotgun-and-rod is a material misrepresentation. Shipping the Sunset Highway hat with a stock pickup instead of a lifted truck dilutes the brand attitude. Both should re-render before product launch.

**Recommended re-render prompt notes:**
- Hat #2: explicitly call out "fishing rod with visible reel and line guides, crossed under a shotgun, cowboy hat at intersection" and stress *asymmetric* silhouettes for the two implements.
- Hat #3: explicitly call out "lifted truck with exaggerated tire-to-fender gap" + "dust plume trailing rear tires" + "backlit silhouette against sun disk".
