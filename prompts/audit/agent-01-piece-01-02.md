## Piece 01 — Member 001 tee

### Mockup: public/images/products/capsule-01/piece-01-member-001.jpg
- showsFullGarment: full
- whiteRectangleDefect: severe
- printScale: too-large (rectangle reads ~13"×17", fills most of the back panel — the *type* itself is roughly the right footprint, but the rectangle around it inflates the visual hit)
- printPlacement: correct (back panel, upper-center, top edge starts roughly where the brief specifies)
- garmentColorMatchesBrief: off (the tee shows as a medium grey-charcoal — not "Pepper washed near-black." It looks more like a heather grey than the pigment-dyed Pepper colorway specified)
- paletteCompliance: has-pure-white (the bounding rectangle behind the print is unambiguously pure white — not Creek Cream `#F5F0E1`. The brief says pure white is forbidden anywhere)
- consistencyWithCapsule: inconsistent (this is a Printful ghost/flat mockup; other capsule pieces look like the same generator but the white-rectangle defect appears across multiple pieces — see Piece 02)
- verdict: kill
- verdictReason: A pure-white rectangle is plastered onto the garment with the artwork inside it — this is the exact failure mode the brief was written to prevent, and the tee color is wrong too.
- observed: A charcoal-grey crewneck tee shown from the back, flat on a white background. A sharp-edged rectangular pure-white panel is laid across the upper back/shoulder area, and the entire charter-crest typography ("CHARTER MEMBER / BAD DECISIONS · GOOD TIMES / HOODLUMS / SINCE TWO THOUSAND TWENTY-FOUR / NO. 001 OF THE FIRST EDITION") sits inside that rectangle in distressed gold and faded cream. The rectangle has crisp 90-degree corners and does not blend into the fabric — it looks like a sticker slapped on the shirt, not a discharge print bleached into pigment-dyed cotton. The brief specifies cream `#F5F0E1` type on the bare Pepper tee (no panel), with the discharge process making the ink sink into the cotton. What was delivered is the opposite: an opaque pure-white substrate hosting the type.

### Artwork: artwork/capsule-01/piece-01-back.png
- pngTransparency: has-white-bg (the PNG canvas reads as solid white, not transparent — the cream `#F5F0E1` brief copy would be nearly invisible against transparency on a white viewer, and the canvas here has a definite light/white field. This is almost certainly why the mockup ended up with a white rectangle: the artwork was composited as a flat PNG with white background instead of a true alpha-channel transparent file)
- paletteCompliance: has-other-violation (the "cream" elements — CHARTER MEMBER, BAD DECISIONS GOOD TIMES, SINCE TWO THOUSAND..., NO. 001... — render as a desaturated greenish-tan tone, not the specified Creek Cream `#F5F0E1`. The gold HOODLUMS wordmark is closer to spec but reads more as a yellow-ochre `#B8860B`-adjacent than the warmer `#C9A227` listed for the back print)
- briefMatch: structurally yes, executionally weak. The composition is correct — top arch "CHARTER MEMBER", subhead "BAD DECISIONS · GOOD TIMES", massive blackletter "HOODLUMS" centered, hairline rule, "SINCE TWO THOUSAND TWENTY-FOUR", "NO. 001 OF THE FIRST EDITION" at bottom. Distress is present. Layout reads like a saloon-charter certificate as briefed. But the cream type is far too low-contrast / muddy to read on a near-black Pepper tee in real life — it'd disappear into the fabric. And the file appears to NOT be on a transparent background, which is the root cause of the mockup defect.
- verdict: re-render
- observed: A vertical typographic crest with five text elements stacked top-to-bottom on what appears to be a near-white background, not transparent. The "HOODLUMS" wordmark in distressed gold-ochre blackletter is the visual focal point and is the strongest element. The four cream/tan typographic lines surrounding it are too faded and yellow-tinged to read as Creek Cream — they'd flatten into the Pepper fabric. The distress texture and saloon-charter geometry are on-brief; the color execution and background transparency are not.

## Summary
- Total mockups audited: 2
- Severe defects found: 2 (Piece 01 mockup + Piece 02 mockup — both exhibit the pure-white rectangle defect)
- Recommended action: Both mockups should be killed. The root cause is upstream: both artwork PNGs were saved with a white background instead of true alpha-channel transparency, and the mockup generator is faithfully compositing that white field onto the garment as a visible substrate. Fix the PNGs first — re-export `piece-01-back.png` and `piece-02-front.png` with genuine transparent backgrounds (not just light-colored bg). Then re-generate both mockups. Beyond the transparency fix, Piece 01's artwork needs the cream type pushed toward the actual `#F5F0E1` Creek Cream value so it survives a discharge print on Pepper; Piece 02's artwork is missing gold grillz, missing the "EST · MMXXIV · CAPSULE 01" rope-inscription, and missing the CH monogram on the bandana knot — all three are brief-specified identity details. Piece 01's mockup tee color also reads as charcoal-grey rather than Pepper washed near-black; verify the mockup generator was given the correct Comfort Colors 1717 Pepper variant. Until the PNG transparency is fixed, every Printful mockup in this capsule will reproduce this defect — this is not a per-piece styling problem, it is a pipeline problem.
