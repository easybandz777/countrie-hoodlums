# Audit Report — Agent 02 — Pieces 03 + 04

## Piece 03 — Last Light, County Line (artwork only, no mockup yet)

### Artwork: artwork/capsule-01/piece-03-back.png

- pngTransparency: has-white-bg (RGB mode, NO alpha channel; corner pixels read ~RGB(253,253,253) — effectively pure white)
- paletteCompliance: has-pure-white (the surrounding background is pure white #FDFDFD, not Creek Cream #F5F0E1; the artwork's interior colors — antique gold, cream, bronze, deep-green sunset — appear on-palette)
- briefMatch: Partial. The scene itself nails most of the brief — lifted pickup on a dirt ridge with tailgate down, two seated figures on the tailgate facing away, glowing bonfire to the left, massive oak tree with branches arching over the top on the right, power lines crossing the upper third, sunset gradient sky with a perfect antique-gold sun disc halfway down, blackletter "WHERE THE PAVEMENT ENDS" + cursive "& the good times start." subtitle. HOWEVER, the brief specifies the entire composition must live inside a "solid matte-black #0A0A0A knocked-out rectangle" that quarantines the artwork from the tie-dye chaos — this PNG instead uses an ornate scalloped/cartouche frame with rope-style edges, NOT a clean black rectangle. That changes the entire concept: the brief's "matted frame around a gallery print" tie-dye logic depends on a hard rectangular block; a scalloped cartouche will let the tie-dye chaos bleed into the silhouette of the print and defeat the whole two-pass screenprint rationale. Also missing the "HCC CAPSULE 01" bottom-right corner marker the brief calls out.
- verdict: re-render
- observed: Scene composition and color palette are strong and faithful to the cinematic-tintype brief — this is genuinely good artwork. The two blocking problems are structural: (1) the PNG ships on a pure-white background with no alpha, which violates both the "fully transparent background" print spec and the capsule's "no pure white anywhere" hard rule; (2) the brief is explicit that the artwork must sit inside a rectangular matte-black knockout panel, but this file delivers it inside a scalloped/ornamental cartouche, which breaks the tie-dye quarantine strategy that the entire piece is engineered around. Both fixable in re-render — keep the scene, ditch the cartouche, deliver as RGBA on transparent background.

The navy-blue mark also directly violates the locked palette's hard prohibition on navy. This cannot ship.

Piece 03 additionally needs the ornate cartouche replaced with the brief-specified rectangular matte-black knockout panel; without that change the entire tie-dye-quarantine concept that the piece is built on collapses. Net: 3 re-renders required, zero ship-ready assets in this batch.
