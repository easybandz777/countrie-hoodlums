## CAPSULE STRATEGY

**Name:** DIRT ROAD HEIRLOOMS / VOL. 01
Volume-numbered, not seasonal. VOL. 02 drops when the work is ready — not when a calendar says so.

**Tagline:** *Built to outlast the night that earned it.*

**Thesis:** The most expensive thing isn't new — it's earned. Every piece is engineered to look like it already has a story before it leaves the box. The blanks are heavyweight, the dyes are washed, the embroidery sits like it's been there a season. We are not selling clothing; we are selling artifacts from a life the buyer wants to have lived.

The through-line is **patina** — visual, emotional, material. Nothing in this capsule looks new. Nothing looks like it's trying. That's the flex.

## FAILURE MODES (the strategist's pre-mortem)

1. **Graphic fatigue.** If more than 5 of the 9 pieces have heavy front-and-back hits, the rack reads as a graphic-tee dump, not a curated capsule. The minimalist pieces (6, 8, 7) MUST stay quiet — protect them from designers who get nervous about empty space.
2. **Color drift.** A single piece in burgundy, navy, or pure white kills the entire capsule's coherence in the lookbook. Reject anything outside the 4-color rule even if it's beautiful in isolation.
3. **Trying too hard to look "country."** Cowboy fonts on every piece, horseshoes everywhere, "y'all" written out → becomes Yellowstone cosplay and forfeits the streetwear half of the brand. Country has to be *implied through context* (lookbook, materials, palette), not shouted on the garment.

## Top 3 red flags (must resolve before artwork finalized)

1. **Discharge print commitment (7-day deadline).** If you accept DTG substitutes on the 6 RED/YELLOW pieces, the capsule becomes "premium-priced DTG merch" — price-to-value collapses on first wash. Cost of getting wrong: ~$60-90K in returns + brand-reputation damage. Verify Printful's 2026 capability via support ticket THIS WEEK, then commit to Path C for the 6 RED pieces.

2. **Piece 5 hood-top print feasibility (14-day deadline).** Hood-top printing requires un-assembled panel screenprinting (industry term: "blank-stage printing"). Most POD hoodies arrive pre-sewn — you cannot retrofit. Either source panels from a manufacturer who prints pre-construction (Bayside, Royal Apparel — Made-in-USA shops) OR drop the hood-top print entirely. The HOODLUMS ONLY hood-top is the piece's signature.

3. **Tackle-twill on Piece 9 (14-day deadline).** Tackle-twill "24" is the hero front detail of the Homecoming sweatshirt. Stahls' and similar varsity shops do this for $18-28/garment in 200+ qty, with 4-6 week lead times. Commit a vendor now; falling back to embroidered "24" on flat satin keeps the visual but loses the dimensional distinction.

# ADDENDUM — PRE-MORTEM & CUSTOMER SERVICE PLAYBOOK

## 8 specific failure modes (with prevention)

1. **Site crashes at 7pm CT drop.** Probability: HIGH. Impact: $$$$. Prevention: Static-export PDPs + homepage, move cart/checkout to Stripe Checkout (their infra), k6 load-test at 15K virtual users two weeks out, Cloudflare Waiting Room set to 500 concurrent on checkout, pre-warm CDN 30 min before drop.

2. **Payment processing fails for $300+ carts.** Probability: MED. Impact: $$$. Prevention: Email Stripe support 10 days pre-launch with launch volume + AOV forecast, enable 3DS for >$200, raise Radar thresholds, idempotency keys on every checkout, hold inventory in cart for 10 min not on charge-success.

3. **Wave I sells out in <60 seconds.** Probability: MED-HIGH. Impact: $$$ (long-tail reputation). Prevention: Cloudflare Turnstile on add-to-cart, 1-per-SKU per account, account-required checkout (no guest on flagship), 5-min cart hold post-add, "Charter Reserve" 10% holdback released 72 hrs later by waitlist.

4. **Wave I DOESN'T sell out.** Probability: MED. Impact: $$ (narrative damage > revenue). Prevention: Model conservative sell-through (3-5% list conversion) NOW, right-size run quantities BEFORE marketing claims scarcity, never publish total unit count, frame as "edition of [SKU-count]" not aggregate, plan a Wave II surprise drop of a non-promised SKU if Wave I lags.

5. **First 50 pieces ship with duplicate numbers.** Probability: MED. Impact: $$$ (trust on the one promise that matters). Prevention: Physical numbered tag affixed to garment AT receiving from Printful, stamp matches tag, photo logged before pack-out, two-person verification on first 25 of any SKU, scan-to-confirm before shipping label prints.

6. **Printful changes a blank's availability mid-fulfillment.** Probability: MED. Impact: $$$. Prevention: Email Printful account manager 14 days pre-launch confirming SKU + size run reservation, identify a verified-equivalent blank for each item pre-launch, allow yourself in T&Cs to substitute to an equivalent with customer opt-out.

7. **Influencer post backfires on launch day.** Probability: LOW-MED. Impact: $$. Prevention: Vet seed list with basic-due-diligence pass, don't send pieces until 5 days out, no exclusivity contracts so you can quietly decline to amplify.

8. **PR narrative hijacked ("rich kid larping country culture").** Probability: MED. Impact: $$$ (only if mishandled). Prevention: Founder bio + About page must already contain real story — where you're from, who's making this, why. Pre-write founder response. Don't argue in QRTs. Pin Charter Roll once it has 100+ real names from real places.

## CS playbook — the two hardest moments

**(A) "This shirt looks worn and dirty. I paid $X for this?"**

WRONG: *"Hi [name]! Thanks so much for reaching out! We're so sorry you're disappointed! Discharge printing is a unique process that creates a vintage aesthetic..."*

RIGHT: *"That's the discharge print working correctly — it strips dye out of the garment instead of sitting on top, which is why it reads soft and slightly uneven. It's the same reason it won't crack or peel. If you'd like, I can send a couple of close-up shots from our shop so you can compare. Wear it once before you decide — almost everyone changes their mind. If you still don't want it after that, we'll figure something out."*

**(B) "I wanted 001 and got 247. This is bullshit."**

WRONG: *"Numbers are assigned randomly and we cannot accommodate specific requests. We apologize for any inconvenience."*

RIGHT: *"Numbers go out in checkout order, full stop. 247 means you were the 247th person through. There's no version of this where we hand 001 to whoever asks loudest — that's the part that would actually be bullshit. Same piece, same Roll, same forever. If you want a refund, say the word and 247 retires."*

## Returns policy (numbered editions)

**Non-numbered pieces (caps, pins):** 14 days from delivery, unworn, tags attached, full refund.

**Numbered pieces:** Size exchanges only, within 14 days, unworn, edition stamp intact. The original is destroyed and logged. Your number does not change. Your place on the Charter Roll does not change.

**Returns close the number.** When a numbered piece is returned outside a size exchange (defect, refund), that edition number retires permanently. Not re-stamped, not re-sold, not re-assigned. The Charter Roll marks it "RETIRED — [date]." This is the only fair version of "limited edition."

## The one system to build pre-launch (if you had one week)

**The numbering ledger + scan-to-ship station.** Simple Airtable + iPad at the pack bench: scan the garment's paper number tag, the row pulls up the customer + assigned number + stamp photo requirement; you can't print the shipping label until the photo is attached and a second initial is logged. Every other failure mode — site crash, sellout rage, PR storm — can be apologized for and survived. A duplicate #047 destroys the one promise the entire capsule is built on, and the only thing that prevents it is a system that physically cannot let it happen. Build the railing before you walk the edge.
