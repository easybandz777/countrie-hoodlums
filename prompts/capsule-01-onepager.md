## THE FIVE COHERENCE RULES

1. **Hand-stamped edition number** on inside neck label, matte gold — every piece. Format: `VOL. 01 / 047 of 200`.
2. **Antique Gold #C9A227** must physically appear on every piece.
3. **Same woven cotton side-seam label** on every premium piece: *DIRT ROAD HEIRLOOMS · VOL. 01 · EST 2024 · CH*.
4. **Same hangtag system:** kraft paper + waxed olive cord + gold-foil CH stamp + edition number on reverse.
5. **No pure white anywhere.** Every "white" is Creek Cream `#F5F0E1`.

---

## THE CHARTER ROLL

Every buyer's edition number, locked against their name in a members-only registry on the site forever. Buyer #001 stays #001 for as long as this brand exists. Returns retire the number permanently. Public sell-through counter ("047 of 200 sold") is the scarcity proof. Names visible to members only.

**The Charter Roll is the moat.** Competitors can't copy it because they'd be Number 2 by definition.

## THE BIG RISKS, NAMED

1. **Discharge print not available on Printful.** 6 of 9 pieces need to route off-platform to a discharge specialist (LA Apparel + screenprint partner). DTG substitutes will visibly age wrong by month 3.
2. **Hand-stamping = the show-stopper operation.** Printful won't do it. In-house workflow needed: Reiner B6K + VersaCraft Real Gold ink, ~4 min/piece, ~13 hours for the 200-unit flagship. Two-person verification + photo log per garment.
3. **Charter Roll integrity.** Single duplicate-number destroys the only promise the entire capsule is built on. Build the scan-to-ship station before launch — physically cannot let it happen.
4. **Dead-stock at 30% sell-through ≈ $145K.** No-restock means no markdown clearance without burning the scarcity story. Tightened caps protect the downside.
