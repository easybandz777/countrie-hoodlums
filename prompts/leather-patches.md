## Production Notes — for Hobbs Peak's laser-engraving team

- **Patch dimensions:** ~3.0" wide × 2.0" tall (76mm × 51mm). Rounded corners (~3mm radius).
- **Leather stock:** Vegetable-tanned cowhide, 3–4 oz weight. Natural medium-brown tannage; **avoid pre-dyed leather** so the burn contrast is clean.
- **Laser settings (CO2, ~60W):** ~85% power / 350 mm/s for the deep deboss on the brand text and main icon; ~50% power / 400 mm/s for finer arch text and decorative borders. Two passes at lower power produce a cleaner edge than one high-power pass.
- **Vector source:** Each AI mockup below is for *visual reference only* — re-trace the design as clean vector (SVG/AI) before sending to the laser. DALL-E will mangle the text slightly in the mockup; the production-ready file uses a real font (Western serif like *Smokum* or *Cabin Sketch*, or a chunky display sans like *Bungee*) — pick once and use across all 6 patches for consistency.
- **Patch attach:** 4 corner stitches in natural cotton thread (#69 bonded) directly through the front panel before crown assembly.
- **Color rule:** All engravings are **single-color** (the burned tone of the leather itself). No fills, no overlays. The design works because the leather darkens where the laser passes.
- **Brand recognizability test:** Squint at every design from 6 feet away — you should still see "COUNTRIE HOODLUMS" and ID the brand. The whole point of these is the name on the patch.

**Image-gen workflow:**
1. Paste each prompt into a fresh ChatGPT chat (DALL-E 3). Use a **new chat per prompt** — DALL-E does better with clean context, and rapid back-to-back image gens get rate-limited.
2. Save the output to `~/Downloads/` with the filename listed under each prompt.
3. Tell Claude "done" — I'll WebP-convert, generate cartoon companions, and wire all into the shop.
